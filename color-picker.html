Supported Color Formats

  • HEX: The standard web format — six-digit hexadecimal prefixed with #. Used in CSS, HTML, and design tools everywhere.
  • RGB: Specifies colors by red, green, and blue intensity (0–255). The native model for screen-based design.
  • HSL: Hue, Saturation, Lightness — the most intuitive model for creating color variations and harmonious palettes.
  • RGBA: RGB with an alpha (opacity) channel. Essential for overlays, transparency effects, and layered UI design.

Color Theory: Palette Types Explained

Triadic: Three colors equally spaced around the color wheel (120° apart). Creates vibrant, balanced palettes with strong contrast between all three hues.

Analogous: Colors adjacent on the wheel (±20°). Creates cohesive, natural-feeling designs — ideal for calm, harmonious interfaces.

Complementary: Two colors directly opposite each other (180° apart). Maximum contrast — great for bold CTAs and emphasis against neutral backgrounds.

How to Use

  1. Pick a Color: Click or drag on the SV canvas to set saturation and lightness. Drag the hue bar to change the hue. Or type directly into the HEX field.
  2. Copy a Format: Hit Copy next to HEX, RGB, HSL, or RGBA to grab the value for your code or design tool.
  3. Generate a Palette: Use the Triadic, Analogous, or Complementary buttons in the sidebar to build a multi-color scheme.
  4. Extract from Image: Upload any photo or graphic — the tool analyzes it and surfaces the most dominant colors as clickable swatches.
  5. Save to History: Click Save to store the current color. Your history persists between sessions and holds up to 12 colors.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this color picker completely free?

Yes — no limits, no subscriptions, no watermarks. Use it as many times as you like.

Are my images uploaded to your servers?

No. All color extraction and picking happens entirely in your browser. Nothing ever leaves your device.

What image formats work for color extraction?

JPEG, PNG, WebP, GIF, BMP, and most other common formats. Images up to ~50MB work well; very large files may be slow depending on your device.

How many colors can I extract from an image?

Up to 8 dominant colors per image using a bucket histogram algorithm that identifies the most visually significant hues.

How many colors can I save in history?

Up to 12. The oldest entry is removed when the limit is reached. History is stored in your browser's local storage and clears if you clear browser data.

Which browsers are supported?

All modern browsers — Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Safari, and Opera. The tool requires HTML5 Canvas support, which every major browser has had for years.
